SPRING

Spring, also known as springtime, is one of the seasons, succeeding winter and preceding summer. There are various technical definitions of spring, but local usage of the term varies according to local climate, cultures and customs.

SUMMER

Summer is the hottest season, occurring after spring and before autumn. At or centred on the summer solstice, the earliest sunrise and latest sunset occurs, daylight hours are longest and dark hours are shortest, with day length decreasing as the season progresses after the solstice.

MONSOON

The monsoon season (sometimes called the rainy season) is the time of year when most of a region's average annual rainfall occurs. Generally, the season lasts at least a month. Areas with wet seasons are dispersed across portions of the tropics and subtropics.

AUTUMN

Autumn also known as fall marks the transition from summer to winter. Autumn is the season when the duration of daylight becomes noticeably shorter and the temperature cools considerably.

WINTER

Winter is the coldest season. It occurs after autumn and before spring. The tilt of Earth's axis causes seasons; winter occurs when a hemisphere is oriented away from the Sun.
